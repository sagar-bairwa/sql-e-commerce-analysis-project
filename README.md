\# 🛒 E-Commerce Sales Analysis using SQL



\## 📌 Project Overview



This project focuses on analyzing an E-Commerce dataset using Microsoft SQL Server to uncover valuable business insights related to customers, products, orders, and revenue.



The analysis is divided into multiple stages, starting from data understanding and basic exploration to advanced business analysis and final insights generation.



A total of 25 SQL queries were written to answer real-world business questions and improve analytical problem-solving skills.



\---



\## 🎯 Business Objectives



The goal of this project was to answer important business questions such as:



\- Who are the highest-value customers?

\- Which products generate the most sales?

\- Which product categories perform the best?

\- How does revenue change over time?

\- Which cities contribute the most customers?

\- What business insights can help improve sales performance?



\---



\## 🗂 Dataset Information



The project uses four interconnected tables:



| Table Name | Description |

| customers | Customer information |

\## 🔍 Key Business Insights



\### Customer Insights



\- Customer concentration varies significantly across cities.

\- A small group of customers contributes a large portion of total revenue.



\### Product Insights



\- A few products dominate total sales volume.

\- Product performance differs significantly across categories.



\### Revenue Insights



\- Revenue shows clear monthly trends and fluctuations.

\- Sales opportunities can be identified through trend analysis.



\### Business Recommendations



\- Focus retention efforts on high-value customers.

\- Increase inventory planning for top-selling products.

\- Promote underperforming categories strategically.

\- Monitor monthly revenue trends for forecasting and planning.
